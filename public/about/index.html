About ZKNOT.

ZKNOT is an independent educational project on physical trust — the question of how we know that the object in front of us is what it claims to be, and the systems, visible and invisible, that decide whether we believe it.

The project exists because most of the security education available to the public is about the digital world: passwords, phishing, encryption, the threats that arrive through a screen. The physical world, where the cables and the chips and the packages and the seals actually live, is treated either as an industrial specialty for engineers or as a source of clickbait panic. Neither of those treatments serves the curious reader. This project is an attempt at a third register.

The Three Properties

ZKNOT, as an enterprise, lives in three places at once. Each has its own purpose, audience, and voice. We list them here because the distinction matters and because we would rather be honest about it than have anyone wonder.

ZKNOT, Inc.

zknot.io

The company. Commercial products, licensing, enterprise and government customers. Where the work is paid for and the patents are filed.

Verifyknot

verifyknot.io

The platform. Consumer-facing verification tooling — the place a person goes to check whether a physical object is what it claims to be.

ZKNOT

zknot.org

This project. Education, documentation, the longer view. Funded by the company, editorially independent of it.

Editorial Independence

This site is funded by ZKNOT, Inc. The company pays the writers, the editors, and the hosting bill. We say so plainly because pretending otherwise would be ridiculous: the names share a letterhead.

What we mean by editorial independence is more specific than financial separation. We mean: essays are not chosen because they make a commercial product look good. Topics are not avoided because they touch a competitor. Where ZKNOT, Inc. has a direct commercial interest in a subject we are writing about, we say so in the body of the essay, not in the fine print. We do not run sponsored content. We do not accept payment to cover a topic. If we ever do any of those things, we will say so before we do them, in plain language, on this page.

How We Write

We write for a reader who is curious and intelligent but not a specialist. We assume our reader has a high-school physics education and the patience to read for ten or fifteen minutes at a stretch. We do not assume our reader has an engineering degree, has heard of the relevant standards, or knows the jargon. Where we use a technical term, we define it the first time it appears.

We try not to be alarmist. The cheap version of writing about hardware security is to make every essay sound like the reader's pocket is about to be attacked. The honest version is harder, because the honest version requires distinguishing between what is structurally possible and what is practically likely. We try to do the harder thing.

Every essay is dated, numbered, and corrigible. If we are wrong about something, we want to know. Corrections are noted at the bottom of the relevant essay. Write to editors@zknot.org.

Who

ZKNOT, Inc. was founded by William Shane Wilkinson and is based in Salt Lake City, Utah. The educational project is currently edited by the founder, with the intention of bringing on outside contributors as the project matures. If you are a researcher, journalist, or engineer who would like to contribute, write to editors@zknot.org.

What We Are Not

We are not a nonprofit. We are not a standards body. We are not, despite the .org, a 501(c)(3). The domain is a signal of intent — that the work on this site is meant to serve the public understanding of these subjects rather than to sell anything — but it is not a tax status. We say this so no one is misled by the suffix.

We are also not a news site. We do not chase developments. We do not have a publication schedule. We publish an essay when an essay is ready. The cadence is irregular, by design.
